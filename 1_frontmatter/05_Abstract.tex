\chapter*{CONTINUAL LEARNING AND CATASTROPHIC FORGETTING IN LANGUAGE MODELS FOR VIETNAMESE LAW\\ABSTRACT}

Large Language Models (LLMs) reason strongly across domains, including legal question-answering and inference, but keeping their knowledge temporally valid is hard: statutes are amended, regulations change, and confidential or repealed content must be removed. When legal knowledge is ingested \emph{continually}, Continual Pre-Training and Continual Instruction Tuning update weights directly and therefore suffer catastrophic forgetting and weight drift, while unlearning specific facts from weights is costly and hard to verify.

This work proposes a RAG-centric, non-parametric framework for Vietnamese legal LLMs that keeps the base model frozen and delegates facts to an external store. A selective memory module applies Ebbinghaus-based consolidation and decay to shrink the index and reduce noise while a compliance hard-gate guarantees that in-force clauses are never omitted; temporal/compliance gating combining a Retriever Policy ($P$) and a Prompt Constraint ($Q$) handles knowledge update and controllable unlearning through one mechanism; and lightweight LoRA adapters supply legal reasoning behaviour without storing facts. The central contribution is \emph{not} a new RAG algorithm but quantitative evidence---on Vietnamese law and across three tasks---that this frozen-base RAG resists catastrophic forgetting where parametric tuning collapses.

The framework is evaluated under continual ingestion against parametric (CPT/CIT) and semi-parametric (ReGrad) baselines at the \emph{same} small model scale on question answering, natural language inference, and syllogistic reasoning, with test scenarios drawn from data-rich legal sub-domains such as labour and tax law. Four falsifiable hypotheses are examined: parametric collapse (H1), RAG forgetting-resistance with Backward Transfer near zero (H2), selective memory preserving recall as the store grows (H3), and a task-dependent reasoning ceiling---retrieval helping QA substantially but tapering at syllogism (H4). The approach offers compliant, low-cost, instantly updatable legal assistance with no factual weight drift.
