\section{Contributions of the Study}

The primary contributions of this study to the fields of legal artificial intelligence and large language model continual learning are summarized as follows:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Legal Importance-Based Selective Memory with Compliance and Temporal Gating:} We introduce a retrieval ranking score designed specifically for the legal domain, extending cognitive models of memory importance and forgetting (the Ebbinghaus curve) to legal text units so the system down-ranks outdated or rarely accessed regulations while a strict compliance hard-gate guarantees in-force legislation is never decayed or missed. Folded into the same non-parametric module is the temporal/compliance gating---including the $P+Q$ unlearning filters for replaced laws, sealed cases, and personal data---so that knowledge update and controllable forgetting share one mechanism.
    \item \textbf{Quantitative Evidence of Forgetting-Resistance under Continual Ingestion (core):} We provide, for the first time on Vietnamese law and across question answering, natural language inference, and syllogistic reasoning, empirical evidence that a frozen-base RAG framework resists catastrophic forgetting (Backward Transfer near zero and non-decreasing performance as ingested knowledge grows) where parametric continual learning (CPT/CIT) collapses, benchmarked at the \emph{same} small model scale against a semi-parametric baseline (ReGrad). This reframes catastrophic forgetting in legal LLMs as a measurable, falsifiable contrast rather than an assumed property.
    \item \textbf{Standardized Vietnamese Legal-CL Benchmark and a Reasoning-Ceiling Finding:} We establish a reproducible benchmark protocol with metrics for retrieval accuracy, catastrophic forgetting, time-regime adaptation, and unlearning leakage on public Vietnamese datasets---VLQA \parencite{nguyen2025vlqacomprehensivelargehighquality}, ViLegalNLI \parencite{duong2026vilegalnlinaturallanguageinference}, and LegalSLM \parencite{le-etal-2025-overview}---and characterize the \emph{task-dependent reasoning ceiling}: retrieval substantially helps question answering, but its benefit tapers towards syllogistic reasoning, which remains bounded by the frozen base model.
\end{enumerate}
